Belly Button Biodiversity Dashboard

When is the last time you washed your belly button? In a studied published in PLOS ONE, researchers found 2,368 species of bacteria in just 60 belly button samples. Today our wesite will allow you to explore some test subjects who allowed us to test their belly buttons for what bacteria was contained with in them. The dataset reveals that a small handful of microbial species (also called operational taxonomic units, or OTUs, in the study) were present in more than 70% of people, while the rest were relatively rare. We hope this will allow all who see it to consider spending some extra time to make sure they are washing themselves properly to help prevent infections or any vicious bacteria to grow.
